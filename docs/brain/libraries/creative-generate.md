# `src/lib/ads/creative-generate.ts`

Turns a fully-backed [[creative-brief]] into an actual static ad via [[gemini]] `generateNanoBananaProCombine` (Nano Banana Pro). The [[creative-brief]] is the brain; this is the hand. Deterministic prompt assembly from the brief's structured fields — the acquisition hook leads, retention truths ride in the body, proof is real, and price appears ONLY via an allowed treatment. See [[../reference/meta-scaling-methodology]].

## `generateCreative(workspaceId, brief, opts) → GeneratedCreative`

Returns `{ buffer, mimeType, prompt, expectedCopy }`. `expectedCopy` is the exact `{ headline, offer, trust }` the QA pass must verify renders un-garbled.

`buildPrompt(brief, hasDesignRef)` assembles the Nano Banana prompt:
- **Headline** — for an **own-brand** angle: `brief.angle.hook`, rendered EXACTLY (the anti-garble instruction). For a **competitor imitation** (`compositionTransfer` + a design ref): the competitor hook is the angle to ECHO, but the model writes OUR headline in the same structure naming ONLY our product — because the competitor's raw hook often carries THEIR brand/product name (e.g. "MUD\WTR Mushroom Tea Blend"), and rendering it verbatim over our packshot is a brand mismatch the QC gate correctly rejects (2026-07-13). For an imitation, `expectedCopy.headline` is set to `brief.productTitle` (QC verifies OUR name renders ungarbled) instead of the competitor string.
- **PRODUCT FIDELITY (2026-07-13)** — the pack is reproduced faithfully from the provided product image; the model must NOT invent or garble packaging micro-text (ingredient icons, fine print). Nano Banana redrawing a pack from a reference hallucinates gibberish labels ("Cocoa Flaspert Hand lens", "35 SeUngs") — a real defect. **ALL** text anywhere must be real, correctly-spelled words. The [[creative-qa]] `textLegible` check (and the `creative-qc` box skill) now scope PACKAGING text explicitly.
- **No-transformation guard (2026-07-13)** — when the brief carries no transformation, the prompt HARD-FORBIDS any before/after / weight-loss / results-timeline panel. Nano Banana free-associated a fabricated weight-loss before/after on competitor imitations (QC-rejected); the explicit negative stops it wasting a generation on an auto-reject.
- **Transformation** = the brief's single real reviewer, quote + name rendered EXACTLY (a genuine review). See the image rule below.
- **Supporting** = 1–2 retention truths as a small secondary line.
- **Trust bar** = first 4 of `brief.proofStack`.
- **Offer** = shown ONCE as a single badge (`offer.headline`), optionally the per-serving value or the strikethrough+disclaimer. **Never a bare MSRP** (hard rule).
- **REVIEW FIDELITY (2026-07-17)** — a competitor design reference often carries THEIR customer review / testimonial / star-rating, and on a composition transfer the model copied their review words verbatim (a fabricated testimonial — their words, not about our product). The prompt now carries a `REVIEW FIDELITY` hard rule: any review/quote/reviewer-name/rating in the reference is THEIRS, never render it; **render ONLY our provided review** (a real, FEATURED review of our product), OR — when no review is provided — render NO review/testimonial/rating at all. The AI MAY tighten a long featured review to its strongest lines (a faithful condensation), but the reviewer NAME stays exact and no claim is invented. Sourcing: [[creative-brief]] `buildCreativeBrief` now PREFERS a **featured** review (`product_reviews.featured`, via `pi.reviews.featured` / the `byClaim` featured flag) for `leadProof`, favoring one that also backs the angle's benefit — so the review the model swaps in is a curated featured review. Pinned by `src/lib/ads/creative-generate.test.ts` (provided-review vs no-review branches).
- **CLAIM FIDELITY (2026-07-17)** — every product attribute / ingredient / nutrient descriptor rendered anywhere on the image must be TRUE of our product (per the packshot + brief). The imitate-then-innovate flow risks carrying over the COMPETITOR's product descriptor — the 2026-07-17 Amazing Coffee test rendered "New daily **protein** coffee" on the stories placement (imitated from a competitor's protein-coffee ad) even though Amazing Coffee has no protein; the feed placement correctly said "superfood coffee". The old imitation headline clause only stripped the competitor's BRAND, not a false ATTRIBUTE. The prompt now (1) tells the imitation HEADLINE clause to DROP any non-true competitor attribute and SWAP IN our real product nature, and (2) carries a `CLAIM FIDELITY` hard rule (peer to OFFER FIDELITY) forbidding "protein"/"keto"/"collagen"/etc. unless our product actually is that. `buildPrompt` is exported + pinned by `src/lib/ads/creative-generate.test.ts`. (Author-mode CAPTIONs are separately guarded by the [[never-fabricate]] firewall, but the IMAGE renders before copy-authoring, so it needs its own guard.)
- **OFFER FIDELITY (2026-07-16)** — the ONLY discount / percent-off / dollar-off / free-shipping / BOGO / "X for $Y" claim allowed **anywhere** on the image is `brief.offer`. The prompt now carries a hard rule forbidding the model from inventing / echoing / carrying over a *different* discount number from a headline, subhead, or badge — and when no offer is supplied, the ad must show NO discount claim at all. Paired with the sanitization pass in [[creative-brief]] `sanitizeCompetitorHook` (which strips a competitor hook's baked-in "50% OFF" before it ever reaches this prompt), together they close the 2026-07-14 Amazing Creamer contradiction where the headline screamed "50% OFF" while our offer badge said "Up to 34% off".
- **NO COMPETITOR OFFER — render-side (2026-07-18)** — the copy-side no-competitor-leak gate only inspects text; a leak that lives in the PIXELS (a competitor's 'free tote' offer graphic baked into the Feed 4:5 render) passed unseen while the copy stayed clean — the 2026-07-18 Superfoods Tabs regression. Three defensive layers, all in `creative-generate.ts` and inherited by every format render (Feed / Reels / Stories / Feed-JPG all share `buildPrompt`, so a fix here reconciles the paths that leaked with the ones that didn't): (1) `stripCompetitorOfferArtifacts` scrubs freebie artifact tokens (`free tote` / `free gift` / `free bag` / `bonus item|gift|pack|tote|bag` / `gift with purchase` / `gwp` / `giveaway` / bare `tote`) out of the imitation HEADLINE before it becomes the "the proven competitor angle to ECHO is …" quote — twin of `sanitizeCompetitorHook` in [[creative-brief]] but scoped to graphic artifacts instead of discount numbers, and only applied on the imitation path where competitor DNA feeds the prompt; (2) the prompt carries a `NO COMPETITOR OFFER` hard rule (peer of `OFFER FIDELITY`) that negative-prompts the model with the same token names by intent, naming every artifact explicitly so Nano Banana knows what NOT to paint; (3) `renderPromptHasCompetitorOffer(prompt)` is a pure deterministic guard that scans the composed CONTENT above the hard-rules block (`NEGATIVE_PROMPT_MARKER = "OFFER FIDELITY (hard rule)"` splits content from negative rules so the guard doesn't false-positive on its own enumeration) — a hit on the composition-transfer path throws `RenderPromptCompetitorOfferError`, refuses to hand the prompt to Nano Banana, and rides the existing `qa_or_gen_failed` regen path in [[creative-agent]] for another attempt instead of shipping the leak. Pinned by `src/lib/ads/creative-generate.competitor-offer-render.test.ts`.

`opts.designReferenceUrl` passes a proven winner as the FIRST image to match its design language; `opts.compositionTransfer` = a competitor imitation (reuse layout, swap ALL content to ours). The regenerate-on-fail cap ([[creative-agent]] `MAX_QA_ATTEMPTS`) is **3** (bumped from 2 on 2026-07-13) so the stricter packaging-text QC has room to land a clean take instead of starving the batch count.

### Image guardrails (the grey-area line)
- The **quote + reviewer name must be a REAL review** — never invent a name, alter a quote, or add a fake "verified purchase" badge.
- A **before/after transformation image MAY be AI-generated** when the real reviewer submitted no photo (CEO-accepted risk, 2026-07-10) — but it **must be PHOTOREALISTIC**: an actual-photograph look (real skin texture, natural lighting, candid-from-home framing), **never a cartoon / illustration / drawing / 3D-CGI render**. The 2026-07-10 first pass rendered a cartoon couple; the prompt now hard-forbids illustration style in both the transformation clause and the HARD RULES line.
- The story must be **self-consistent**: one reviewer, one number, headline = caption = photo person (avoids the "84 lbs headline / 63 lbs caption / third person's photo" bug).
- Only `http(s):` / `data:` image URLs are passed to Gemini; relative storage paths are filtered out (they can't be fetched).

## The QA pass (caller's job)
The Ad Creative Agent (a Max-session lane) calls `generateCreative`, then **visually QAs** the returned bytes against `expectedCopy` — garbled/dropped text, any fabrication, a bare price, a duplicated offer badge, or a cartoon transformation — and **regenerates on fail** before landing it in [[media-buyer-agent|Bianca]]'s ready-to-test bin.
