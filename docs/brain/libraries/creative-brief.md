# `src/lib/ads/creative-brief.ts`

The **brain of the Ad Creative Agent** (the tool that keeps [[media-buyer-agent|Bianca]]'s ready-to-test bin stocked). Grounded entirely in the [[product-intelligence]] SDK, so every claim is verifiable by construction — the reason generated creatives can **auto-feed** the bin with no human gate. See [[../reference/meta-scaling-methodology]] (angle model + price-on-static rule).

## The two-axis angle model (the anti-commodity engine)

`selectAngles(pi, transformationStories)` scores every candidate angle on **two independent axes** and ranks for a COLD/prospecting creative:

- **acquisitionPower (0–10)** — does it stop a stranger's scroll + earn the first buy? Boosted by: a specific **transformation** ("I lost 30 lbs"), an **objection/skeptic** frame (the format that won 2026-07-09), **curiosity/pattern-interrupt** ("the diet industry doesn't want you to know"), a **differentiated high-intent** benefit (weight, brain fog, cravings).
- **retentionTruth (0–10)** — does the product deliver it so well it *keeps* them? High for the loved experience benefits (energy-no-crash, taste) and high-frequency review clusters.

**The trap it exists to avoid:** *"energy without jitters / no 2pm crash"* is the **#1 review cluster** (retentionTruth ~10) but a **commodity** acquisition angle — every coffee claims it, so it converts no stranger. `COMMODITY`-matched angles are **demoted** (acquisitionPower −3) and never lead; they surface as **supporting body copy** instead. Verified 2026-07-10: for Amazing Coffee the top angles rank transformation/weight/objection/curiosity; no-crash sinks out of the top 12 and reappears only in `supportingBenefits`.

Candidates come from `product_ad_angles` (ready hooks), the biggest real transformation reviews, and review clusters (whose frequency feeds `retentionTruth`). Deduped by hook, ranked `acquisitionPower desc`.

**No internal angle-category label in rendered copy (2026-07-19/20):** an angle's `leadBenefit` doubles as a grouping label for some sources — the ingredient-angle builder tagged its `leadBenefit` the literal `"Ingredient / mechanism"` (and the transformation builder `"Weight loss (real customer transformation)"`). But `supportingBenefits` maps angle `leadBenefit`s into the on-image subhead, so those category labels **leaked into the rendered pixels** ("No jitters, no crash, Ingredient / mechanism") and Max's copy-QC rejected the creative — on BOTH own-brand and competitor runs (the subhead is angle-source-agnostic). Two-layer fix: (1) the ingredient-angle builder now uses the ingredient's REAL mechanism/benefit as `leadBenefit`, never the label; (2) `INTERNAL_ANGLE_LABELS` / `isInternalAngleLabel` (exported) filter any residual category label out of `supportingBenefits` before it can render. Pinned by `src/lib/ads/creative-brief.test.ts`.

**Curated LEAD BENEFIT seed ([[../specs/dahlia-hooks-riff-competitor-angle-and-weave-in-lead-benefit]] Phase 1, 2026-07-18):** the very first candidate `selectAngles` emits comes from `pi.benefits` where `role='lead'` — the per-product curated differentiated acquisition angle. Its hook is the first punchy `customer_phrases` entry (or `benefit_name` when the phrase list is empty); its `leadBenefit` IS the `benefit_name`, so `buildCreativeBrief` resolves a real `leadProof` from `pi.reviews.byClaim(benefit_name)` downstream. Seeded with `acquisitionPower=10` and `commodity=false` so the diversification pass ranks its `source:'benefit'` group first and the `commodity || retentionTruth>=8` supporting-benefits filter can't demote it to body copy. Missing `role='lead'` row degrades gracefully — no seeded angle is emitted and today's ad_angle/transformation/ingredient/review-cluster pool is unchanged. Fixes the class of defect where Amazing Coffee's cold creative led its headline with a purely borrowed commodity hook (`"Tired of the coffee jitters?"`) with the product's actual differentiated benefit (weight loss, science- and customer-confirmed) never even a candidate on the table. Pinned by `src/lib/ads/creative-brief.test.ts` (`selectAngles —` blocks).

## The fully-backed brief

`buildCreativeBrief(pi, angle, transformationStories)` → `CreativeBrief`: the hook + **leadProof** (a real review via `byClaim`, or the transformation story itself for a weight angle, or an ingredient citation) + **transformation** (real reviewer + their before/after photo) + **supportingBenefits** (the retention truths for body copy) + **proofStack** (award/certs + store selling points) + **offer** (rendered as an *allowed* price treatment — strikethrough+disclaimer or per-serving-vs-latte, **never bare MSRP**) + **imageRefs** (before/after · hero · packshot) + **guardrails** (attestations that nothing is fabricated).

**RIFF — weave the lead benefit into a competitor imitation (`brief.leadBenefitWeave`, [[../specs/dahlia-hooks-riff-competitor-angle-and-weave-in-lead-benefit]] Phase 2, 2026-07-18):** whenever the driving `angle.source === 'competitor'`, `buildCreativeBrief` also stamps `leadBenefitWeave: { benefitName, softPhrasings }` on the brief, populated from `pi.benefits` where `role='lead'`. The `softPhrasings` array is the benefit row's `customer_phrases` verbatim (already grounded — `"feel lighter"`, `"lost weight"`, `"curbs my appetite"`) so Dahlia has a naturally-spoken vocabulary without inventing one. Consumed by the `dahlia-copy-author` SKILL's RIFF rule: the authored hook must BLEND the competitor's proven framework (from `competitorDna`) with our lead benefit — the CEO's north-star shape `"Tasty coffee, feel lighter, no jitters"` for Amazing Coffee vs a pure-borrow `"Tired of the coffee jitters?"` that leaves the differentiator absent. `buildCreativeBrief` accepts an opt-out `{ pureCompetitor: true }` for the MINORITY explore slot per batch: [[creative-agent]] `stockProduct` calls `markPureCompetitorMinoritySlot` to flag AT MOST ONE competitor slot as pure (only when the batch already carries ≥2 competitor slots, and the LAST slot in the pool so the top-ranked riff still leads) — that's the "keep a minority explore for learning" rail. A brief with `leadBenefitWeave: null` (own-brand, no role='lead' benefit at all, or the minority pure slot) obeys the pre-Phase-2 IMITATE-DEBRANDED rule as-is. Pinned by `src/lib/ads/creative-brief.test.ts` (`buildCreativeBrief — RIFF …`) + `src/lib/ads/creative-agent.test.ts` (`markPureCompetitorMinoritySlot`).

**Convert a competitor's BENEFIT into OURS (dahlia-converts-competitor-benefits-to-ours, CEO 2026-07-21):** the benefit twin of the offer-slot swap. When Dahlia imitates a competitor whose product has benefits OURS does NOT (a collagen brand sells `gut · immunity · hair · nails`; Amazing Creamer's real listed benefits are `skin · focus · weight`), she kept carrying the competitor's benefits into the copy and the never-fabricate firewall correctly rejected them (`firewall_claim_miss: supportingBenefit:claim_not_in_source` — exhausted with no ad). Fix has two halves: (1) `buildCreativeBrief` now appends OUR product's REAL listed benefits (`pi.benefits` / `product_benefit_selections` where `role ∈ {lead, supporting}`) into `brief.supportingBenefits` (after the retention-truth base so the image subhead `.slice(0,2)` is unchanged, deduped, capped 8). Because `verifyClaimTrace` grounds a `supportingBenefit` claim against `brief.supportingBenefits`, a claim about one of OUR listed benefits now PASSES the firewall — and a benefit carried from the competitor (not in OUR list) still fails, which is what forces the conversion. (2) The `dahlia-copy-author` SKILL's IMITATE-DEBRANDED `mechanism_claim` rule now says **CONVERT THE BENEFIT TO OURS** — keep the competitor's hook shape + framework, SWAP their benefit for OUR real one, never claim a benefit our product lacks. Pinned by the `supportingBenefits surfaces OUR listed product benefits` case in [[../../../src/lib/ads/creative-brief.test.ts]].

**Convert a competitor's PROOF NUMBER into OURS (dahlia-grounds-proof-numbers, CEO 2026-07-21):** the THIRD of the imitate-then-innovate conversion trio (offer → risk-reversal/proof · benefit → ours · **proof stat → OUR real number or qualitative**). After the benefit-convert landed, the Bloom→Amazing Creamer imitation moved from `claim_not_in_source` to `firewall_claim_miss: supportingBenefit:fabricated_number` — Dahlia was now using the RIGHT benefit (skin/focus) but INVENTED a stat to keep the source ad's proof punch. The `dahlia-copy-author` SKILL's IMITATE-DEBRANDED `proof` slot rule now says **CONVERT THE PROOF, NEVER INVENT A NUMBER**: if the competitor's proof is a stat (`%` / count / timeframe / rating), either cite one of OUR real `proofStack` numbers (`700,000+ customers`, `15,000+ reviews`, the money-back guarantee, a real award) with `source='proofStack'`, or make the proof qualitative and drop the number. Any number not tracing to OUR brief (`proofStack` / a real `reviews.byClaim` body / an `ingredients` dosage) is a `fabricated_number` firewall miss. SKILL-only reinforcement of the existing rail-1 (Numbers) + rail-6 (proofStack) grounding — the firewall behavior is unchanged (pinned by `src/lib/ads/never-fabricate.test.ts`).

**Swap a competitor's OFFER slot for one of OUR grounded selling points ([[../specs/swap-competitor-offer-slot-for-our-grounded-proof-benefit-or-feature-in-debrand]] Phase 1, 2026-07-18):** a competitor's offer slot is often an OFFER we do not run (free tote / free gift / bonus item / discount) — carrying it through the debrand into Dahlia's imitation rubric fails EVERY downstream gate. On Superfood Tabs' first run, `firewall_claim_miss: leadProof` blocked the ungrounded free-tote claim; after the firewall-recovery fix, `author_cold_offer_leak_post_gate` bounced it — both gates were correctly refusing an offer we don't run. The CEO's fix is upstream: do NOT drop the winning angle and do NOT carry the competitor's offer — SWAP the offer slot for one of OUR grounded selling points (a proof point like `"700,000+ customers"`, a benefit like `"reduce bloating"`, or a feature like `"15 superfoods per serving"`). `buildCreativeBrief` now runs `isCompetitorOffer` from the [[debrand]] library over the raw offer slot (and the raw hook) whenever `angle.source === 'competitor'`; when it matches, the offer is replaced with `chooseGroundedSubstitute({ proofStack, supportingBenefits, leadProof, productFeatures })` (returns `null` when the brief carries no grounded selling point — the offer slot is nulled) and the hook is scrubbed with `stripCompetitorOffer` so the winning STRUCTURE (`"with product held up outdoors"`) survives without the freebie. The substitute chooser prefers `proofStack` proof points (awards / certs / brand-wide selling points — the strongest verified evidence) → `supportingBenefits` (retention truths) → `leadProof.text` → `productFeatures` (a last-resort pool populated from `pi.ingredients.length`, e.g. `"15 superfoods per serving"`). **The brief's REAL offer (`brief.offer`, populated from `pi.offer` with an allowed price treatment) is a different type entirely and is NEVER touched** — only the competitor's un-runnable offer is swapped. A non-offer text in the source row's offer field (e.g. `"Try it risk-free for 30 days"`) passes through untouched. Own-brand angles never enter this path. `creative-agent.ts` `stockProduct` re-runs the detector as a DEFENSE-IN-DEPTH pass at debrand time so an offer that slipped past upstream (e.g. one revealed only after the brand token was stripped) is still caught before Dahlia's session sees it. Pinned by the tests in [[debrand]] and the `buildCreativeBrief — a 'Free tote' competitor offer is SWAPPED …` / `… brief.offer (our REAL offer …) is UNTOUCHED …` cases in [[../../../src/lib/ads/creative-brief.test.ts]].

**Preserved competitor copy DNA (`brief.competitorDna`, dahlia-preserve-competitor-copy-dna-debranded Phase 1):** whenever the driving `angle.source === 'competitor'`, `buildCreativeBrief` stamps a `competitorDna: { hook, framework, mechanismClaim, proof, offer, competitorAdvertiser }` field on the brief, populated from the underlying [[../tables/creative_skeletons]] row (threaded via `angle.raw` by `stockProduct`'s competitor-angle mapper — no second DB read). The four slots are the same market-tested words the rival's 45+ paid days already proved; carrying them onto the brief is what lets Dahlia's author-mode session imitate the winner's structure instead of collapsing to a generic supporting benefit. The RAW hook (pre-sanitizer) is preserved so the author session can see the winner's original words before debranding at author time. **Deterministic-mode `buildMetaCopy` is untouched** — the field is opt-in for author-mode consumers only. The M2 [[never-fabricate]] firewall's `verifyClaimTrace` reads the same slots when a claim cites `source='competitorDna'`. The pure `debrandForOurBrand(text, competitorAdvertiser, ourBrand)` helper Dahlia calls per slot lives at `src/lib/ads/debrand.ts` — null-safe, whole-word case-insensitive brand-token strip with a small product-name allowlist (`coffee`/`tea`/`mud`/`drink`/`creamer`/`matcha`) that prevents over-strip on advertiser tokens like "Ryze Coffee". Pinned by `src/lib/ads/debrand.test.ts`.

**Unified-breakdown surfacing (`brief.conceptTags`, [[../specs/dahlia-researches-from-winners-flow-ad-library]] Phase 1):** whenever `angle.source === 'competitor'`, `buildCreativeBrief` also stamps `conceptTags: { angle, archetype, why_it_works, cialdini_lever, awareness_stage, format }` on the brief. The breakdown comes from OUR winners-flow vision on the underlying [[../tables/creative_skeletons]] row (LANE A + LANE B both emit this shape via [[../creative-skeleton]] `parseSkeleton`), threaded onto the [[creative-brief|ScoredAngle]] as `angle.conceptTags` (also mirrored on `angle.raw.conceptTags`) by [[creative-agent|Dahlia]]'s `stockProduct` competitor-angle mapper. Own-brand angles leave the field null. Consumed by Dahlia's copy-author session as the imitation rubric ("what archetype + cialdini lever won?") alongside `competitorDna`, and by Max's Phase 2 QA grader as the benchmark for competitor-selection + temperature-fit grading (a cold-audience task's winner-concept `awareness_stage` should be `unaware` / `problem_aware` — a mismatch scores lower on Max's competitor-selection axis).

**Competitor-hook discount sanitization (`sanitizeCompetitorHook`, 2026-07-16):** a `source:'competitor'` angle's hook comes from a rival's live ad — and a competitor often bakes their promotional NUMBER right into it (`"MUD\WTR Mushroom Tea Blend — 50% OFF"`, `"Save 40% Today"`, `"Free Shipping · Best Coffee"`). Reused verbatim as OUR headline, that number lands on our creative and **contradicts** the real offer the same brief renders from `brief.offer` — the 2026-07-14 Amazing Creamer bin draft screamed **"50% OFF"** in the headline while its own offer badge said **"Up to 34% off"**, the exact false-claim / proxy-optimizer failure the north star warns about. `buildCreativeBrief` now scrubs percent-off / dollar-off / `free shipping` / BOGO / `X for $Y` tokens out of `angle.hook` **before** it becomes part of the brief, so every downstream consumer (`buildMetaCopy` here, `buildPrompt` in [[creative-generate]], `expectedCopy` fed to [[creative-qa]]) reads from a single already-sanitized source. Own-brand hooks are untouched. The generation prompt also carries a matching **OFFER FIDELITY** hard rule so the model can't free-associate a different discount either — the ONLY discount rendered on the image must come from `brief.offer`. Pinned by `src/lib/ads/creative-brief.test.ts`.

## The Meta ad copy — `buildMetaCopy(brief)`

`buildMetaCopy(brief)` → `{ primaryText, headline, description }` — the ad TEXT Dahlia publishes alongside the render, composed from the SAME grounded brief so caption and image match. **Fixes the 2026-07-13 defect** where the copy was `headline = the OFFER (truncated to "Up to 34% off + free shipping (25% Subsc")`, `primaryText = hook + a benefit fragment ("I lost 40+ pounds! Appetite suppression/craving control")`, `description = empty`:

- **headline** = the hook/benefit, **never the offer** (the offer belongs in the description). Capped at `META_CAPS.headline` (40). For competitor imitations, the headline is a de-branded supporting benefit — **preferentially one carrying a [[lf8|Life-Force-8]] term** — so the ad leads with a cold-scroll stopping power (energy, focus, proven, family, etc.) that directly satisfies the ads-supervisor gate.
- **primaryText** = a real DR caption on separate lines: a **proof-led opener** (the transformation/review quote — always OUR customer's words) → a **benefit line** (`{product} — {two supporting truths}`, with [[lf8|LF8]] terms ordered first) → the **trust stack** → the **offer + a soft CTA** ("… Shop now 👉"). Capped at `META_CAPS.primary_text`.
- **description** = the allowed price treatment (per-serving-vs-latte or the offer headline), never empty. Capped at `META_CAPS.description`.
- **De-brand safety:** a `source:'competitor'` angle's raw `hook` can carry the COMPETITOR's brand/product name ("MUD\WTR vs Ryze") — the image de-brands it, and so must the copy. For a competitor angle the headline falls back to a de-branded benefit and the opener leads with OUR review; the raw hook NEVER reaches the copy. (Guarded by `scripts/build-meta-copy.test.ts`.)
- **LF8 guardrail (Phase 1, 2026-07-14):** the composed `headline + primaryText` must carry **at least one [[lf8|Life-Force-8]] term** whenever the brief carries an LF8-adjacent supporting benefit. The pass prefers LF8 terms in both the headline and the benefit line (`orderedSupporting.sort` by [[lf8]] presence). **If the composed copy still carries no LF8** (e.g., all supporting benefits are non-LF8 and the competitor hook de-brands away) **a final injection fires:** the pass prepends an LF8-carrying supporting benefit line so the caption satisfies the [[ads-supervisor]] gate by construction. **Escape hatch:** when the brief truly has **zero LF8-adjacent language** anywhere (no supporting benefits match [[lf8|LF8 keywords]], no offer headline), the copy is left as-is — the supervisor re-flags on the next pass (human-facing path: "product intelligence is LF8-thin"). Never fabricates a benefit. Pinned by 4-check test suite in `src/lib/ads/creative-brief.test.ts`.

**`META_CAPS` ([[ad-tool-config]]) raised 2026-07-13:** `primary_text` 125 → **600** (125 was the "…See more" fold length used as a HARD cap — it forced story-less captions; the fold is a display detail, not a reason to gut the copy), `description` 30 → **90**; `headline` stays 40. The [[ad-validator]] fatal-caps and the [[ad-angles]] LLM prompt read the same constants, so every copy path loosens together. **Raised again 600 → 1200 on 2026-07-18** to reconcile with the long-form 3-paragraph rail ([[creative-agent]] `validateCopyParagraphStructure`, hook + 2-3x body + close) — at 600 the two rails were mutually unsatisfiable on some angles and Dahlia's revise loop exhausted with `validator_failed: meta_caps` (Ashwavana Zen Relax). 1200 comfortably fits a real 3-paragraph long-form primary and stays well under Meta's ~2200 truncation limit; a runaway 3000-char primary still fails, so the cap remains a real ceiling. Coexistence pinned in `src/lib/ads/copy-validator.test.ts`.

**Cold-offer rail lives at the PERSISTER, not here (dahlia-audience-temperature-marking-and-cold-offer-gate Phase 2):** `buildMetaCopy` remains **temperature-agnostic** — under Advantage+ the creative IS the audience selector, but this pass has no `audience_temperature` input and never should (it composes copy from the grounded brief). The deterministic rail refusing a cold-audience creative whose caption leaks offer/price language lives one step later, at [[creative-agent]] `insertReadyCreative` — the SOLE bin-insert chokepoint — which reads the row's `audience_temperature` and runs [[lf8]] `hasColdOfferLeak` before writing the [[../tables/ad_campaigns]] row. Placing the rail at the persister (a) keeps `buildMetaCopy` pure, (b) makes the refusal atomic (no half-written row to unwind), and (c) leaves the M1 Max QC free to catch subtler mismatches without owning the offer-leak class probabilistically.
