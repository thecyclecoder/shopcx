## The accumulated coaching to bake in (now archived as rolled-into-mandates)
- **When When materializing the fix spec and enqueuing its build, the coverage-register path lets a commit/author failure terminate the job with no diagnosed verdict — either an uncaught throw ('no verdict') or a bare needs_attention with a one-word error — so the registry entry is never queued and the coverage gap silently persists.:** At the START of every coverage-register job, wrap the whole materialize-spec → enqueue-build sequence (markNewSpecInReview + the build-job insert) in a try/catch, and on ANY failure resolve to an EXPLICIT verdict that writes a diagnosis + remediation path (what step failed, the concrete error, and whether to retry or escalate to the owner). Never let authorSpecRowFromMarkdown's MissingVerificationError escape as a tooling 'no verdict', and never park at needs_attention with just 'empty spec body' / 'no fix spec body' — always record why the entry couldn't be committed and how to unblock it, because the rubric fails whenever a real coverage gap is left unresolved with no evidence of fallback reasoning. — builder-worker.ts:2703 calls markNewSpecInReview un-guarded, and markNewSpecInReview re-throws MissingVerificationError at builder-worker.ts:6117 — so a spec-commit failure escapes runCoverageRegisterJob entirely (job 8accf808 died as 'agent produced no verdict'), while the guarded branches at 2685/2699 only park at needs_attention with a one-word error; the entry itself was actually correct (id matches the createFunction at acquisition-research-cadence.ts:79), so the miss was purely the un-diagnosed, un-escalated commit-failure path, not the entry content.
- **When When a spec commit or registry write fails, the worker marks the job 'needs_attention' and stops, leaving the coverage gap unresolved with no retry, fallback, or documented escalation path.:** When you see a spec commit or registry write failure, do NOT simply emit 'needs_attention' and exit — instead, (1) retry the write at least once with diagnostic logging, (2) if the retry also fails, produce a partial registry entry or a structured failure record documenting the intended entry, the error, and the explicit remediation step required, then escalate that artifact as your output. — The rubric requires either a correct registry entry OR no coverage gap missed; a silent 'needs_attention' exit satisfies neither condition. Providing a documented failure artifact with a remediation path proves the failure was diagnosed and keeps the coverage gap visible to the next handler, closing the accountability loop even when the write itself cannot succeed.
