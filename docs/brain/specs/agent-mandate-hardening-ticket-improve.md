# Harden the Ticket Improve agent — roll persistent coaching into its mandate

**Owner:** [[../functions/platform]] · **Parent:** [[platform-director-agent]] — graduate persistent coaching into a durable fix (director grades agents: low score → fix spec, never a CEO escalation)
**Found in use 2026-07-01:** the **Ticket Improve** agent (`ticket-improve`) sits at **6.1/10** after **2** coaching attempts that didn't stick. Rather than escalate to the CEO, roll the coaching into a permanent fix so the agent improves at the mandate level.

## The accumulated coaching to bake in (now archived as rolled-into-mandates)
- **When Every low-graded turn spends its budget on platform root-cause diagnosis and a ticket→spec proposal (journey-delivery.ts, improve-actions.ts, remediation.ts, the workflow action type) and closes without ever surfacing the mandatory change_summary envelope, so graders can only see a completed no-op; the two worst turns also ended on an open 'option A or B, which do you want?' question instead of a plan.:** When you finish investigating a ticket, before you write a single word of code/spec diagnosis, FIRST build and lead with the change_summary envelope the mandate requires — text_diff (before/after), field_changes (category/tag/status old→new + rationale), tags_added/removed, categorization_rationale, and customer_voice with a quoted preserved phrase (or set no_changes:true with a one-line note for pure-investigation turns). Only after that proof exists may you attach AT MOST ONE ticket_spec for a platform bug you spotted, and always finish with a resolve_sequence closeout as a concrete proposed plan — never end a turn with an open 'which do you want?' question; propose the smallest correct plan and let the human approve or redirect. — ticket-improve/SKILL.md:57-92 (agent-mandate-hardening-ticket-improve) makes change_summary mandatory and states verbatim (line 59-60) that 'a completed turn with no diff is indistinguishable from a no-op and caps the rubric at ~6/10' — which is exactly the ceiling all 10 grades hit (six 6s), because every log_tail shows only a `problem`/`fix` spec body (cb3064f2, 7afa5432, 954a592f) or an open routing question (3b30118f, 5d7209a0) and zero change_summary; leading with the SKILL.md:66-75 envelope and proposing a plan per SKILL.md:53 gives graders the before/after they say they can't see, lifting the observable-improvement ceiling.
- **When Every low-graded ticket-improve turn drifts into a platform/code bug root-cause hunt (journey-delivery.ts portal branch, improve-actions.ts vs action-executor.ts, remediation.ts escalation guard, Appstle lineId) and ends its final JSON without the mandatory change_summary envelope — and the two worst (jobs 3b30118f, 5d7209a0) end on a bare open question to the founder ('Which do you want?') with no plan, no closeout, no rubric evidence.:** At the START of every job, treat it as an EDITORIAL closeout, not an engineering ticket: decide the category/tags/reply and fill change_summary as you investigate. End EVERY turn with a valid reply-or-propose JSON whose change_summary is populated — text_diff (before/after snippet), field_changes, tags_added/removed, categorization_rationale — or, for pure-investigation turns, no_changes:true with a rationale (SKILL.md:61-90). NEVER end on an open question to the founder; if you need a decision, still propose the smallest correct plan and let them decline. When the root cause is a platform/code bug, do NOT let the bug-hunt replace the deliverable: still complete the category+tags+customer reply closeout and fold the bug into a SINGLE ticket→spec action within the same plan. — A change_summary-presence probe over all 8 surviving log_tails returned change_summary:false and no_changes:false for every one, so graders saw no before/after, tag delta, or categorization rationale — exactly the '6/10, can't verify the rubric' complaint; SKILL.md:61-90 makes that envelope mandatory on every reply/propose. The two 4/10 jobs' final assistant JSON is a lone {"message":"…Which do you want?"}/{"message":"…are you firing the executor?"} with no closeout — the editorial job was abandoned mid-investigation, which the change_summary/closeout discipline directly prevents.
