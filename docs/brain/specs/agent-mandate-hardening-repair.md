# Harden the Rafa agent — roll persistent coaching into its mandate

**Owner:** [[../functions/platform]] · **Parent:** [[platform-director-agent]] — graduate persistent coaching into a durable fix (director grades agents: low score → fix spec, never a CEO escalation)
**Found in use 2026-06-30:** the **Rafa** agent (`repair`) sits at **6.9/10** after **2** coaching attempts that didn't stick. Rather than escalate to the CEO, roll the coaching into a permanent fix so the agent improves at the mandate level.

## The accumulated coaching to bake in (now archived as rolled-into-mandates)
- **When Rafa marks a job 'completed' when a fix exists in name (prior fix, queued build, dismissed label) but has not yet been confirmed as deployed, held, or actually addressing the specific root cause.:** When you see a fix that is 'pending deploy,' inherited from a prior ticket, or only queued — do NOT mark the job completed; instead explicitly state the root cause in your own words, link it to the specific symptom, and either wait for deploy confirmation or hand off with a documented verification gate (e.g., 'verify KPI returns to baseline post-deploy on date X'). — Closing a loop before the fix lands in production means the repair rubric criteria 'fix held' and 'real root-cause' are never satisfied — the job looks resolved on paper but the error can silently recur. Requiring Rafa to articulate the root cause independently (not just inherit a ticket label) and attach a concrete verification step forces both diagnostic rigor and proof that the fix actually held.
- **When Rafa marks jobs 'completed' at the moment a fix is queued or a prior fix is identified, before confirming the deploy landed and the metric/error actually recovered.:** When you see a fix that is 'pending deploy' or inherited from a prior ticket, do NOT mark the job 'completed' — instead explicitly gate closure on post-deploy validation: confirm the deploy succeeded, spot-check the KPI or error signature returned to baseline, and document that confirmation in the log tail before concluding. — The rubric requires 'fix held' and 'real root-cause' — both are unverifiable until production evidence exists. Closing at queue-time or at prior-fix-attribution conflates 'fix submitted' with 'fix confirmed', which is why grades stall at 6/10 regardless of the correctness of the diagnosis itself.

## Phase 1 — bake the coaching into the agent
- Make the above coaching PERMANENT behavior of the `repair` agent — fold it into its prompt/mandate/code (its run-job + prompt in `scripts/builder-worker.ts` and the relevant `src/lib/agents/*`), not ephemeral appended `agent_instructions`. Once baked, the agent should follow it by default.
- Verify the agent's grade rollup recovers (≥ 7/10) over the next window of graded actions; if a coaching class still recurs, the bake-in missed it.
