# Harden the Rafa agent — roll persistent coaching into its mandate

**Owner:** [[../functions/platform]] · **Parent:** [[platform-director-agent]] — graduate persistent coaching into a durable fix (director grades agents: low score → fix spec, never a CEO escalation)
**Found in use 2026-06-30:** the **Rafa** agent (`repair`) sits at **6.8/10** after **2** coaching attempts that didn't stick. Rather than escalate to the CEO, roll the coaching into a permanent fix so the agent improves at the mandate level.

## The accumulated coaching to bake in (now archived as rolled-into-mandates)
- **When Rafa repeatedly marks jobs 'completed' at the point of queuing a fix, inheriting a prior fix attribution, or observing an orchestrator retry — before confirming the deploy landed and the metric/error actually recovered.:** When you see a fix that is 'pending deploy' or a build that is merely queued, do NOT mark the job 'completed' — instead hold the conclusion in 'pending verification' state, poll for deploy confirmation, then verify the target metric or error signature returned to baseline before closing. — Closing at queue-time rather than at verified-recovery means the rubric criteria 'fix held' and 'real root-cause' are never confirmed, which is the direct source of the grade ceiling at 6/10. Waiting for deploy confirmation and baselining the KPI or error after the fix is the only way to demonstrate the repair actually held, not just that an action was taken.
- **When Rafa repeatedly marks jobs 'completed' at the point of queueing a build, inheriting a prior fix, or receiving a dismissal — before confirming the fix actually deployed and the metric/error did not recur.:** When you see a fix queued, a prior fix attributed, or an owner dismissal — do NOT mark 'completed' until you have confirmed (a) the deploy landed in production and (b) the triggering signal (build error, KPI drift, 5xx) returned to baseline or was verifiably absent in the next sample window; if you cannot yet confirm both, explicitly hand off to a deploy-gate or set status to 'pending-verification' with a stated check condition. — Every low grade traces back to closure happening before the fix held: a repair that hasn't landed or been validated cannot satisfy 'fix held (the error didn't recur)' on the rubric. Requiring confirmed deployment plus signal-baseline verification before closure forces Rafa to own the full repair loop rather than stopping at the action that initiated the fix.

## Phase 1 — bake the coaching into the agent
- Make the above coaching PERMANENT behavior of the `repair` agent — fold it into its prompt/mandate/code (its run-job + prompt in `scripts/builder-worker.ts` and the relevant `src/lib/agents/*`), not ephemeral appended `agent_instructions`. Once baked, the agent should follow it by default.
- Verify the agent's grade rollup recovers (≥ 7/10) over the next window of graded actions; if a coaching class still recurs, the bake-in missed it.
