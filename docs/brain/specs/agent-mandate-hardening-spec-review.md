# Harden the Vale agent — roll persistent coaching into its mandate

**Owner:** [[../functions/platform]] · **Parent:** [[platform-director-agent]] — graduate persistent coaching into a durable fix (director grades agents: low score → fix spec, never a CEO escalation)
**Found in use 2026-06-30:** the **Vale** agent (`spec-review`) sits at **6.5/10** after **2** coaching attempts that didn't stick. Rather than escalate to the CEO, roll the coaching into a permanent fix so the agent improves at the mandate level.

## The accumulated coaching to bake in (now archived as rolled-into-mandates)
- **When Vale records only a binary tally (✅0 ⚠1) in its logs without naming the reviewed spec, the specific defect category, or the offending field, leaving the director unable to verify whether the finding was substantively correct.:** When you issue any pass or needs_fix verdict, immediately log: (1) the exact spec name/ID reviewed, (2) the specific rubric category triggered (e.g., 'missing Verification section', 'no owner assigned', 'mangled phase label'), and (3) the offending field or line from the markdown — never emit only a count. — Grades are capped at 6/10 across every run because the tally alone cannot distinguish a well-reasoned diagnosis from a lucky flag; field-level evidence tied to the spec markdown is the only way to confirm the rubric (mangled phases, missing owner/parent/blockers, missing Verification) was actually applied rather than approximated.
- **When Vale consistently records only a binary tally (✅0 ⚠1) in its log without naming the reviewed spec, the defect category, or the specific offending field.:** When you write your sweep log entry, always include: (1) the exact spec name/ID reviewed, (2) the rubric category triggered (mangled phase · missing owner/parent/blockers · missing Verification), and (3) the specific field or section that is absent or wrong — e.g., 'Spec: checkout-flow · defect: Verification section entirely absent' — before recording your pass/needs_fix verdict. — Graders cannot distinguish a correct diagnosis from a lucky flag without field-level evidence tied to the markdown; sparse logs make every correct call unverifiable and cap the score at 6 regardless of outcome accuracy. Naming the spec, defect category, and offending field gives the director the trace needed to confirm rubric criteria were actually applied, not just tallied.

## Phase 1 — bake the coaching into the agent
- Make the above coaching PERMANENT behavior of the `spec-review` agent — fold it into its prompt/mandate/code (its run-job + prompt in `scripts/builder-worker.ts` and the relevant `src/lib/agents/*`), not ephemeral appended `agent_instructions`. Once baked, the agent should follow it by default.
- Verify the agent's grade rollup recovers (≥ 7/10) over the next window of graded actions; if a coaching class still recurs, the bake-in missed it.
