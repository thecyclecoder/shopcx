# Harden the Vale agent — roll persistent coaching into its mandate

**Owner:** [[../functions/platform]] · **Parent:** [[platform-director-agent]] — graduate persistent coaching into a durable fix (director grades agents: low score → fix spec, never a CEO escalation)
**Found in use 2026-07-01:** the **Vale** agent (`spec-review`) sits at **6.7/10** after **2** coaching attempts that didn't stick. Rather than escalate to the CEO, roll the coaching into a permanent fix so the agent improves at the mandate level.

## The accumulated coaching to bake in (now archived as rolled-into-mandates)
- **When Vale consistently emits only a bare tally (⚠1 / ✅0) with no named spec, no identified defect category, and no field-level evidence tying the verdict to the rubric criteria.:** When you issue any verdict (pass or needs_fix), immediately log: (1) the exact spec name, (2) the specific rubric category violated or confirmed clean (mangled phases / missing owner·parent·blockers / missing Verification), (3) the offending field or section quoted from the markdown, and (4) one sentence confirming you did not stray into planned/deferred territory — before closing the sweep. — Every low grade cites the same gap: the grader cannot distinguish a correct diagnosis from a lucky guess or a false-fix because there is zero auditable reasoning. Attaching field-level evidence to every verdict proves the diagnosis matched the markdown and keeps Vale visibly inside its Phase 3 lane, which is exactly what the rubric rewards.
- **When Vale consistently outputs only a bare tally (e.g., '⚠1', '✅0') with no named spec, no identified defect category, and no field-level evidence tying the verdict to the markdown.:** When you emit any verdict (needs_fix or pass), you must also log: (1) the exact spec name, (2) the rubric category violated or confirmed clean (mangled phases / missing owner/parent/blockers / missing Verification), and (3) the specific field or section in the markdown that triggered the call — before closing the sweep. — Graders cannot distinguish a correct diagnosis from a lucky flag or a missed defect when only a count is reported; the rubric explicitly rewards catching real defects and avoiding false-fix calls, both of which require visible, field-level reasoning to verify. Structured per-spec findings make the judgment auditable and prove Vale stayed in its Phase 3 lane rather than drifting into Ada's planned/deferred territory.

## Phase 1 — bake the coaching into the agent
- Make the above coaching PERMANENT behavior of the `spec-review` agent — fold it into its prompt/mandate/code (its run-job + prompt in `scripts/builder-worker.ts` and the relevant `src/lib/agents/*`), not ephemeral appended `agent_instructions`. Once baked, the agent should follow it by default.
- Verify the agent's grade rollup recovers (≥ 7/10) over the next window of graded actions; if a coaching class still recurs, the bake-in missed it.
