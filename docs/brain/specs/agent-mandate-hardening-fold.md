# Harden the Fenn agent — roll persistent coaching into its mandate

**Owner:** [[../functions/platform]] · **Parent:** [[platform-director-agent]] — graduate persistent coaching into a durable fix (director grades agents: low score → fix spec, never a CEO escalation)
**Found in use 2026-07-01:** the **Fenn** agent (`fold`) sits at **6.3/10** after **2** coaching attempts that didn't stick. Rather than escalate to the CEO, roll the coaching into a permanent fix so the agent improves at the mandate level.

## The accumulated coaching to bake in (now archived as rolled-into-mandates)
- **When Fenn begins fold-batch execution without checking quota/infrastructure health first, and produces no structured log of which brain pages were touched, cross-links verified, or archives confirmed — leaving graders unable to validate rubric compliance even when the job nominally succeeds.:** When you see a fold-batch job start, do two things: (1) run a pre-flight quota/health check before any API calls and immediately surface a needs_attention status if the weekly limit is at risk; (2) emit a structured audit entry for every fold action — page name, cross-links set, archive path — so the log tail alone can confirm all three rubric criteria without inspecting the PR. — The repeated 2–5/10 scores come from jobs that either hit the rate-limit with no early warning, or from jobs that 'completed' but left graders with zero visibility into what was actually done. Adding a pre-flight check converts silent quota failures into detectable, recoverable signals; adding a per-fold audit log converts opaque completions into verifiable ones, directly satisfying 'folded into the right brain pages · cross-links correct · archived cleanly'.
- **When Fenn begins fold-batch execution without checking quota/infrastructure health upfront and without emitting structured progress checkpoints or a needs_attention status when a blocking constraint is hit, leaving jobs that fail or succeed with zero auditable evidence of what was done.:** When you see a fold-batch job starting, (1) run a preflight quota/health check as step zero and immediately surface a needs_attention status with a clear reason if any hard limit is exhausted or the environment is unhealthy — do not attempt the fold and silently fail; (2) after every brain page is folded, emit a structured checkpoint line (page name, cross-links set, archive status) so that partial work is recoverable and the log is auditable even if the job is later interrupted. — The rate-limit and orphan-restart failures all bottomed out at zero deliverables because there was no early detection to stop wasted work and no mid-job checkpointing to salvage partial progress — fixing those two habits converts hard failures into recoverable partial successes. The 6/10 completed jobs lost points for the same audit gap: graders could not confirm correct page targeting or cross-link integrity because Fenn never wrote structured evidence into the log, so adding per-page checkpoint lines directly satisfies the rubric's verifiability requirement.

## Phase 1 — bake the coaching into the agent
- Make the above coaching PERMANENT behavior of the `fold` agent — fold it into its prompt/mandate/code (its run-job + prompt in `scripts/builder-worker.ts` and the relevant `src/lib/agents/*`), not ephemeral appended `agent_instructions`. Once baked, the agent should follow it by default.
- Verify the agent's grade rollup recovers (≥ 7/10) over the next window of graded actions; if a coaching class still recurs, the bake-in missed it.
