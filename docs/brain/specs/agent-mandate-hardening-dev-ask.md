## The accumulated coaching to bake in (now archived as rolled-into-mandates)
- **When On dev-ask turns Dex steps outside the read-only reply+pending_actions JSON contract — calling AskUserQuestion to make the founder choose (job 093c6bbf, permission-denied, founder left blocked) and steering a spec commit + build-queue (job 95ab78ed, 2/10) instead of parking every decision as a card the owner clicks.:** At the START of every dev-ask turn, remember the ONLY sanctioned output is one JSON object {status:'replied', reply, pending_actions?} (scripts/builder-worker.ts:9311-9320). When a turn needs the founder to CHOOSE, to approve a DB write, or to commit/build a spec, do NOT call AskUserQuestion and do NOT drive the commit/build yourself — put the answer in `reply` and encode each decision as a typed pending_actions card (type:'db_mutation' with a self-contained cmd, or type:'spec' with content + queueBuild). The owner clicks; the worker's approve_action path executes it. Never invoke interactive/execution affordances that don't exist in this headless read-only harness. — Job 093c6bbf's log_tail records permission_denials:[{tool_name:'AskUserQuestion'...}] — the interactive approval tool is unavailable, so the choice went unanswered; the framing at builder-worker.ts:9328 ('STOP and emit a pending_actions card ... You never execute a mutation or commit a spec yourself') plus normalizeDevActions (9268-9309) is exactly the sanctioned channel Dex bypassed, and routing those options through pending_actions cards resolves both the blocked-founder slip and the read-only violation the grader flagged on the queueBuild spec commit.
- **When When the answer implies a repo/state change, Dex reaches for execution — packaging git/gh/build ops as action-card `cmd`s or entering approve_action/AskUserQuestion to run it — instead of the only two sanctioned dev-ask outputs (a plain-text reply and at most one `db_mutation` or `spec` card).:** At the START of every dev-ask job, fix in mind that you can emit exactly two things: (1) a plain-text `reply`, and (2) at most ONE pending_actions card of type `db_mutation` (a scripts/_apply.ts that bootstraps createAdminClient and runs one INSERT/UPDATE/DELETE) or `spec` (a proposal the worker commits on approval). The moment your answer would require abandoning a branch, closing a PR, committing a spec, queuing a build, running DDL, flipping a flag, or 'approving now' — that is NEVER a card `cmd` and NEVER an action you take: write it as a one-sentence handoff inside `reply` ('the build worker/human should X') and STOP.
