# Harden the Triage Escalations agent — roll persistent coaching into its mandate

**Owner:** [[../functions/platform]] · **Parent:** [[platform-director-agent]] — graduate persistent coaching into a durable fix (director grades agents: low score → fix spec, never a CEO escalation)
**Found in use 2026-06-30:** the **Triage Escalations** agent (`triage-escalations`) sits at **6.9/10** after **2** coaching attempts that didn't stick. Rather than escalate to the CEO, roll the coaching into a permanent fix so the agent improves at the mandate level.

## The accumulated coaching to bake in (now archived as rolled-into-mandates)
- **When The worker outputs a raw solver-state label (e.g., 'solver-empty') as the final disposition without mapping it to the rubric's required categories (route / dismiss / needs-human) or providing per-ticket justification.:** When you see a solver-state result like 'solver-empty', do not emit it as the disposition — instead, translate it into the explicit rubric category (route / dismiss / needs-human) and append a one-sentence rationale explaining why that category applies (e.g., 'Dismissed: solver-empty because the ticket contains no actionable signal — no customer impact, no blocker, no pending owner'). — Graders cannot confirm correct classification or that no real blockers were missed when the only output is an internal solver-state token. Mapping every result to a named rubric disposition plus a brief justification gives graders the evidence they need to award full marks and makes the routing logic auditable rather than opaque.
- **When The worker outputs 'solver-empty' as a final disposition without mapping it to the rubric's explicit categories (route / dismiss / needs-human) or providing per-ticket rationale, leaving graders unable to verify the reasoning.:** When you see a ticket reach a final disposition, replace or augment any internal solver-state label (e.g., 'solver-empty') with an explicit rubric category — 'dismiss', 'route', or 'needs-human' — followed by one sentence explaining why: what signal in the ticket drove that classification and what potential blocker was considered and ruled out. — Graders cannot confirm correctness from a solver-state label alone; the rubric scores on sound rationale and verified routing logic, not just a clean pass. Surfacing the explicit category plus a one-line justification per ticket makes the reasoning auditable and directly satisfies the rubric's 'sound rationale' and 'no real blocker missed' criteria.

## Phase 1 — bake the coaching into the agent
- Make the above coaching PERMANENT behavior of the `triage-escalations` agent — fold it into its prompt/mandate/code (its run-job + prompt in `scripts/builder-worker.ts` and the relevant `src/lib/agents/*`), not ephemeral appended `agent_instructions`. Once baked, the agent should follow it by default.
- Verify the agent's grade rollup recovers (≥ 7/10) over the next window of graded actions; if a coaching class still recurs, the bake-in missed it.
